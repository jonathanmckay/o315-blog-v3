It's been a bit since I made a mistake, and there are definitely some mistakes that I can learn out of this one. At the same time maybe OpenAI post January was a mistake. Should I have left? I don't know, probably not, but the decision was taken out of my hands and it's clear that OpenAI is not interested in growth. There are both tactics and strategy that I can learn, but in the end I just look at it as a card that may have been bad financially but was good in other respects. I got to learn a new company culture, get another jewel into my resume that will be interesting, get a preview into a technology that really is going to change the world, and got paid at least something while I did it.

I now have opinions about where the world is going that I didn't have before, and that can be worth something. What did I learn about myself? That neon is something I'm not willing to give up. I also learned there are a lot of jobs I'm not excited about. I'm not excited to be an RPM. Even being an RPM manager seems like a stretch. I'm not excited about becoming an AI researcher, even though they were the gods worshipped at OAI.

Perhaps more important than my life is the life of Theo and Ren over the last 8 months, as Louisa has made the transition to real estate which is going well overall. That's a big payoff for all the extra time that I spent on it, and it's a model that feels replicable into the future. I enjoyed the library, but didn't enjoy the standups or the meetings as much. The tactical feedback is I could have been tighter on some of the execution meetings, sure.

I think my overall plan is >$1m and we call it fair. $500k - $1m I take the money and then decide. $200k I don't take the money. Ah but dreaming of revenge probably doesn't do me any good. I'll talk to the lawyers, take my best shot, and then move on. It's not like taking OpenAI down a notch is going to help me, but oh boy is it tempting. I don't think I want to be Anthony Pompliano. Focus first on protecting my people and then on protecting myself, then on what I want to do with OpenAI, if anything.

The anger can eat me, what do I do? Talk to lawyers, take my time. I'm 36 with a substantial net worth. I've got another 40 years to do things. The thing is, even if I was at OpenAI, I would have been tented out of the interesting projects over time. So then as a PM you only see 2-3 months in advance. I confidently feel that I'm not missing out on much. I'm not sure this is going to be the next Lime or Snap, but I think it'll look more like the next Twitter.

Setbacks are what makes life interesting. If I'm on a winning streak for too long it's easy to get complacent. So put another way, when the anger is there, pray, take a deep breath, go for a walk. Think about the present, think about the future.

I think this was more of a surprise but in the end will be something that I'm less angry about than Facebook. Certainly something I'm less sad about than Liz, and that situation ended up great. Use this as a system shock to get better, and things will work out well. Use this as a means to stew in anger, and things will get worse. So far I'm 100% with colors since the departure, and I think I can keep doing that going forward.

Not until our net worth dips below 5-10 million, neon is the thing that I think defines who I am and while I'm willing to titrate different aspects of it, if the lure of $50m in the end isn't enough, then I'm not sure that anything will.

**I am a serene bulldozer.**
