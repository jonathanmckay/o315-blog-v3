The war in Ukraine serves to me as a form of proof. It is the revenge of history, and the proof that wars of the past can rear their ugly head in the present, halting progress. Humanity could blow itself up, and it's the war in China that I still believe is the most important and the one where I can apply the most pressure.

What is the meaning of my 7 years at Facebook? I got on the train late, performed to the 1% in what I believe to be my cohort, and doubled my net worth every year. Meta was the elevator into society, my way of paying the entry fee to the American capitalist society. In the same way that the State Department bought me the freedom to consider what I wanted to do next and took me from the lower middle class of early graduation to the 'take my time and pursue excellence' of China. Facebook took me from the 'money would be nice' of middle class to the 'if you want something done right you must build it yourself' mentality of the Silicon Valley elite. That applies to homes, cars, companies and coffee shops. At this point I trust in my ability to do any of those, and with enough time applied, could do them well too.

My time at Facebook domesticated me, and brought me to the state of the art in terms of what it means to build a company, to build teams, interact with press, government, and do so in an international way. When combined with real estate, it was a masterclass on what capitalism means in the 21st century, how a complex organization of people evolves from the start.

It was also the first opportunity to go from observing the world to finding the levers that can move it. To understand what incrementality means, and how hard it is to be a motivating factor of change, how easy it is to convince ourselves we are responsible for the tide.

Meta was a steady ramp of working on increasingly abstract projects and returning to convince myself that they were important through a chain of projects and projects and ties and ties that led to real world change.

And Meta was a second example to see how the world perceives itself so imperfectly, with the power of simple narratives dominating nuance and truth at almost every turn. Perhaps I spent too long here, but I don't see many cases where I should have spent longer.

I see no world in which history or I regret having left in order to live a life on different axes, the question is will I continue to stay in San Francisco? Even if not, at least we have a home base here to return to that is being paid for at a reasonable interest rate.

**Meta was the elite finishing school. And I learned those damn lessons. Now it's time to go out into the world and apply them.**
