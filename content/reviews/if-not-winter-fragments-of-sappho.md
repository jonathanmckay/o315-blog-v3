** 7th book of 2021: Sweet Morsel **
If Not, Winter is lyrical but accessible reminder of universal human experience and how little we can retain of even the best muses. 
 Someone will remember us
          I say
                 even in another time  

Off of paternity leave and back into the office, I'm 
'Looking for a better way to get up outta bed / 
Instead of getting on the Internet / 
And checking on who hit me' 

Video or games are habit forming; doomscrolling is depressing. Books aren't the best place to find distraction, so poetry is where I go. With a bad day at work, Sappho is a perfect afternoon escape. Uncannily, she has advice to avoid angry emails...   
 with anger spreading in the chest
to guard against a vainly barking tongue  

Commentary on what we notice only after lengthy stationary quarantine...
 evening
     you gather back
          all that dazzling dawn has put asunder; 

Remembrance of youthful infatuation...
 you came and I was crazy for you
and you cooled my mind that burned with longing 

...and nostalgia that tugs at the heartstrings. 
 ] you will remember
] for we in our youth
     did these things

yes many and beautiful things 

If all poetry is this good, I should read more.
