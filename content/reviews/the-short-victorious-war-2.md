**The Short Microcosm War**

*The Short Victorious War* isn’t the best Honor Harrington novel, but it’s the most representative—an attempt at Clausewitz in space, with bureaucratic infighting and political vanity, where the reader can safely predict the ending. Weber’s focus on Haven’s mid-level politics isn’t the sharpest, but the sheer scope of topics that he sets up or attempts to touch on in exposition is impressive.

Battles are lopsided by design; Honor always fights with a few hidden advantages. It works, but predictability dulls the edge. Young, the antagonist, is pure caricature.

The *Honor Harrington* series is a three-star epic. This one earns a fourth: tighter, more focused, and still entertaining despite certainty of outcome.
